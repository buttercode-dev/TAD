# The Admin Department Brand System

## Brand promise

**Control the work. See what's due.**

## Brand idea

**Ledger & Signal** combines the familiarity and accountability of a physical admin ledger with the visibility and speed of a modern operating platform.

## Product architecture

- **TAD SaaS** - the customer operates all six departments.
- **TAD Managed** - TAD configures and operates some or all departments.
- **Hybrid** - the customer and TAD divide responsibility department by department.

## Positioning

The complete back-office operating platform for growing businesses.

## Personality

Organised, direct, warm, trustworthy, modern, human and operational.

## Voice

Use plain English and observable outcomes.

Preferred:

- Your invoice queue has 12 records due today.
- TAD runs the workflow. You retain approval and visibility.
- Every open record needs an owner, next action and due date.

Avoid:

- Revolutionary AI automation.
- Effortless transformation.
- Guaranteed revenue or guaranteed collections.
- Vague words such as magic, seamless and intelligent without evidence.

## Core colours

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#111111` | Logo, primary text, high-contrast surfaces |
| Paper | `#F6F1E8` | Main background |
| Card | `#FFFDF8` | Cards, reports and company documents |
| Ledger Lime | `#D8FF57` | Primary active signal |
| Control Blue | `#8DC6FF` | Information and system control |
| Decision Violet | `#C9A8FF` | Approvals and decisions |
| Signal Orange | `#FFB35C` | Exceptions and urgency |
| Success Green | `#5DAE65` | Completed and healthy states |
| Muted | `#6F675D` | Secondary copy |
| Rule | `#DED4C5` | Borders and ledger lines |

## Typography

- Display: Space Grotesk, Arial fallback
- Body: Inter, Arial fallback
- Data and references: IBM Plex Mono, Courier New fallback

## Logo meaning

The two dots represent the human owner and the active signal. The horizontal line represents a controlled workflow or ledger line.

## Required brand language

Use: workspace, department, workflow, queue, owner, next action, due date, decision, report, managed.

Avoid: AI agency, robot, magic, fully automated, guaranteed outcome.

## Graphic language

- Warm paper backgrounds
- High-contrast black typography
- Thin ledger rules
- Small signal dots
- Flat colour blocks
- Operational cards and reports
- Real business photography rather than robots or glowing AI imagery

## Social system

Content pillars:

1. Operational education
2. Pain-point recognition
3. Before-and-after workflow examples
4. TAD SaaS education
5. TAD Managed education
6. Founder and operator credibility
7. Client evidence

Post structure:

**Problem -> operational truth -> practical action -> CTA**

## Company documents

Every company document should use:

- logo top left;
- black and paper colour system;
- thin rule lines;
- clear metadata;
- short headings;
- conventional legal and financial formatting;
- no decorative gradients or unnecessary illustration.

## Digital product rule

The app, website, Client Portal and Admin HQ must feel like the same operating system. Use consistent navigation names, status colours, typography and language.

## Final rule

Everything must feel like one brand: warm paper, sharp black type, one visible signal colour, thin ledger rules, direct language and evidence of work.
