# PROJECT.md — The Admin Department

## Mission

Give small businesses the structure of an admin department without requiring them to hire a full internal team.

## Positioning

The Admin Department is a managed back-office operations service powered by DueToday.

We do not primarily sell access to software. We diagnose the workflow, install the operating method, organise the starting records, help run the daily queue, escalate human decisions and prove the result through weekly reporting.

## Core promise

We turn messy admin into clear, trackable systems — then help the business keep those systems moving.

## Service architecture

### The Admin Department

The customer-facing service company. It sells and delivers:

- Admin Audit
- workflow mapping
- department setup
- starting-data cleanup and migration
- templates and approval rules
- managed daily administration
- weekly reporting
- monthly process improvement

### DueToday

The shared operational platform. It provides:

- private client workspaces
- leads, quotes and invoices
- the Today action engine
- recorded outcomes and next dates
- human approval queues
- TAD operator access across managed clients
- weekly service reports

### Admin HQ

The public workflow demonstration and internal playbook library for:

- Invoice Admin
- Sales Admin
- Client Admin
- Property Admin
- Practice / Booking Admin
- Member Admin

Admin HQ is not the private production database. Protected operational work belongs in the authenticated DueToday/TAD Operations Console.

All six remain part of the service portfolio. They are implemented through one configurable operating method rather than marketed as six unrelated SaaS products.

## Initial commercial offers

1. **Admin Audit** — diagnose the fix-first workflow and controlled pilot.
2. **Department Setup** — configure one complete workflow, organise the starting records and train the team.
3. **Managed Admin** — TAD helps operate the queue, record outcomes, move approvals and produce weekly reports.
4. **Monthly Admin Control** — ongoing review, reporting, template changes and safe automation.

## Operating principles

- Sell organised outcomes, not feature access.
- One workflow first; expand only after the pilot produces evidence.
- Every active record needs a status, owner, next action and due date.
- “Done” must record the real outcome and any next commitment.
- Important customer, supplier and financial actions stay human-approved.
- Protected client information belongs only in approved private workspaces.
- Automation follows a reliable manual process; it does not replace one.
- Never invent savings, recovered revenue or operational results.

## Out of scope until proven through managed pilots

- autonomous payments
- uncontrolled customer messaging
- replacement of accounting systems
- clinical advice or triage
- complex ERP programmes
- broad self-service SaaS expansion

## Current success gate

The next proof is operational rather than technical:

- onboard one consenting managed client;
- configure one department;
- import or capture the real starting backlog safely;
- operate the daily queue for 14 days;
- produce two weekly reports;
- document actions completed, actions rolled over and blockers;
- obtain a clear continue/change/stop decision from the client;
- convert at least one pilot into paid managed support.
